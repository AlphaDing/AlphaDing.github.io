Research Projects

  • Analyzing the BlackLivesMatter movement through Tweets The hashtag #BlackLivesMatter has become a symbol of the anti-police brutality and anti-racism against the Black American movement on Twitter. The work reported in this paper aims to characterize the dynamic developments of the #BlackLivesMatter hashtag and its counter creation #AllLivesMatter on Twitter from 2014 to 2018, by utilizing Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques.
    I am very grateful to be advised by Prof. Brendan O’Connor on this project.
  • Amazon Alexa Automatic ASR Error Correction This project is done while I was an Applied Scientist Intern at Amazon. Alexa is the voice-controlled smart assistant developed by Amazon. The Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) system for Alexa already achieves high accuracy. However, it suffers from long-tail errors such as new comments or long sentences. Thus, we want to develop a model that can automatically detect and correct possible transcription errors made by upstream ASR systems. During my internship, I developed a transformer-based deep neural network model that improved word error rate (WER) by 3.5% compared to the current Alexa production model.
